About Voting in India

What is Voting?

Voting is the process through which citizens select their representatives to form the government. Every citizen expresses their choice by casting a vote for a candidate contesting in an election.

Constitutional Right

The right to vote is provided under Article 326 of the Constitution of India. Every citizen above 18 years has the right to vote without discrimination.

History and Evolution

The first general elections in India were conducted in 1951-52. Paper ballots were used initially and later Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) were introduced.

Who Can Cast a Vote?

  • Citizen of India
  • 18 years or above
  • Registered in voter list
  • Valid voter ID proof

How to Cast a Vote?

  1. Register as voter
  2. Verify electoral roll
  3. Visit polling booth
  4. Verify identity
  5. Press EVM button
  6. Vote recorded securely
